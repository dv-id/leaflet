point

Customers
  • the service user -- is someone who is looking to hire a service to outsource an activity; it could be finding an electrician, personal gym trainer, a tattoo artist or a dog walker, anything really
  • the service provider -- is someone who is professionally providing a service to others; it could be a gardener, oper, wall painter, anyone really
Customer's pain points
  • the service user
    • has no time to fix/resolve a problem by himself
    • is struggling to find a service he needs
    • is uncertain about the quality of a service when provided by someone new/unverified
    • has lack of trust when ordering a service
    • has to go through a complicated scheduling with the service provider
    • lacks planning/scheduling services
    • lacks price and quality transparency with a service provider
  • the service provider
    • is struggling to find customers
    • has a lot of unused capacity
    • has high marketing costs
    • can’t reach outside of his social bubble or his area of residency
    • doesn’t know the prices of other providers in his business
    • lacks information about demand of his service
    • struggles to be on time due to inefficient planning
    • lacks data to improve his service
The Product and Its functions
  • point is a platform that connects service providers with its service customers
  • it provides service users with
    • a list of categories of services
    • a list of service providers
    • a profile of each service provider
    • a scheduling system
    • a map with selected category of service providers
    • a messaging app to communicate with the provider
    • statistics of an average price, dates etc.
  • it provides service providers with
    • a personal profile page
    • a scheduling system
    • a messaging app to communicate with the customer
    • a payment system
    • statistics of average price, demand, location of customers etc.
    • each service provider has a profile with information regarding the provided service such as price, detailed description of the service, available dates, reviews, location, photos, their personal business card, links to other social sites
Platform Screens and User Flow
  • user login
  • user registration
  • service / category search
  • list of providers / map of service providers
  • business card
  • schedule calendar
  • upcoming and historical orders
  • statistics
  • user profile / settings
Business model
  • the platform makes money in three forms
    • firstly from promotions of service providers – they appear near the top of the results or are highlighted in other ways
    • secondly by providing data to service providers – they obtain information about the services they provide such as the average price, demand surges and predictions, costs, customer preferences, improvement tips for a service, geographical distribution of clients, reports, data about their contracts
    • thirdly by processing payments – the platform takes a fee for processing payments
  • (potentially with a subscription model to appeal to the frequent providers/users to have more stable and predictable cash flows, it could implement a freemium model mostly to service providers)
